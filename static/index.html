An Experiment in Language Modeling

I built a transformer from scratch and pre-trained it on ten years of my text messages (two million tokens), with a custom tokenizer that keeps every emoji intact, and masked loss so it only learns to talk like me. The resulting pre-trained model is...bad. Often hilarious, but bad.

To fix that, I built MikeRL. It exposes the model's full token-space decision tree and allows for model observability and interactive, surgical preference alignment: picking good continuations, rejecting bad ones, training with GRPO, rolling back steps that make things worse.

Click on a blue message to try it out!
